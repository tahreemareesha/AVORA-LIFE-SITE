Top 7 Foods That Help You Sleep Better at Night

If different tricks aren't helping you fall asleep, your diet might be the missing piece. Here's what the research says about which foods actually move the needle.

Feb 2, 2026 7 min read

If you feel difficulty sleeping every night and trying different tricks doesn't help, then maybe you should look at your diet. Diet can affect your sleep — and in this post, you'll find the foods that support it naturally.

Why does diet affect your sleep?

What you eat, both before bed and across the day, genuinely shapes how well you sleep. A few reasons why:

  • Foods low in fibre and high in fat and sugar are linked to lighter, less efficient sleep.
  • Sleep itself is directed by hormones in your body.
  • Those hormones depend on specific nutrients to function properly.
  • Those nutrients come from diet — so the right diet supports better sleep.

Seven foods that help you fall asleep faster

Each of these is backed by research on sleep-supporting nutrients.

1. Almonds

Almonds carry two sleep-promoting nutrients: magnesium, which relaxes the body and reduces stress, and melatonin, the hormone that signals it's time to sleep. Eaten as an evening snack, they support the natural sleep cycle.

2. Banana

Bananas contain potassium, which regulates blood pressure and muscle contractions — helping prevent cramps and restlessness during sleep — and tryptophan, an amino acid that converts to serotonin and eventually melatonin.

3. Warm milk

A classic natural sleep aid. Milk contains tryptophan (which becomes serotonin, then melatonin) and calcium, which supports melatonin production. Calcium deficiency has actually been linked to difficulty falling asleep.

4. Oatmeal

Oatmeal is rich in melatonin and complex carbohydrates, which help more tryptophan reach the brain. It's also light and easy to digest, making it a solid evening option.

5. Chamomile tea

Chamomile is rich in apigenin, a sleep-inducing antioxidant that promotes sleepiness and reduces insomnia symptoms, alongside mild sedative effects that calm the nervous system.

Research on chamomile has found improved sleep quality in people dealing with sleep issues and postpartum fatigue.

It's also caffeine-free — a warm, herbal, calming drink that fits naturally into a bedtime ritual.

6. Walnuts and cherries

Both naturally contain melatonin, helping relax the brain and improve sleep. Cherries in particular have shown benefit for people with insomnia and light sleep.

7. Kiwi

A small, low-calorie fruit rich in vitamin C, vitamin E, folate, and potassium — all of which support physical wellness and sleep quality. Some studies show eating one to two kiwis before bed helps people fall asleep faster, sleep longer, and wake less during the night.

How to use these foods effectively

You don't need to eat these right as you're heading to bed — aim for at least two hours beforehand. Pairing them with a wind-down ritual (dim lights, reading) reinforces the effect.

Final thoughts

Not every food works the same for everyone — the honest approach is to try a few and see what your body responds to. These seven are a solid, evidence-backed place to start.
